Hyacinth Macaw

Hyacinth Macaw is the largest Macaw and the largets flying parrot species in the world.

  • Scientific Name :Anodorhynchus hyacinthinus
  • Average Length: 1 meters (or) 3feet 3inches
  • Average Lifespan: 50 years in the wild
  • Habitat: Tropical rainforest habitats in Central and Eastern South America

Hyacinth Macawsâ€™ feather coat is bright blue with gradually becoming lighter towards the head. The most distinctive vibrant yellow ring around the eye and the patch framing the beak make their blue coat stand out. They have long and pointed tails making them the biggest macaw species. They have extremely strong beaks which can crack cocnuts. They are beautiful creatures unfortunately listed as endangered species due to habitat loss and cage bird trading.
